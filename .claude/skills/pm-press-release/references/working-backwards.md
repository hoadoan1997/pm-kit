# Working Backwards Method

## Origin

Amazon's product development process. Start with the press release, work backwards to define what to build.

## Core Principle

If you can't write a compelling press release, the product isn't worth building.

## Press Release Rules

1. **Written for the customer** — not for internal stakeholders
2. **No jargon** — if your grandmother can't understand it, rewrite it
3. **One page max** — forces ruthless prioritization
4. **Future tense** — written as if the product already launched
5. **Specific benefits** — "saves 3 hours/week" not "improves efficiency"

## Internal FAQ Purpose

The FAQ section captures:
- Questions executives will ask about ROI and timeline
- Questions engineers will ask about feasibility
- Questions customers will ask about pricing and availability
- Questions legal will ask about compliance
- Honest answers to "why now?" and "why us?"

## Common Mistakes

- Writing about features instead of benefits
- Using company-internal language
- Making the headline about the company, not the customer
- Skipping the customer quote (it forces empathy)
- FAQ answers that dodge hard questions

## When to Use

- Before starting any new product or major feature
- When pivoting strategy and need to validate direction
- When pitching to stakeholders who need customer-centric framing
- To align cross-functional teams on "what are we actually building?"
