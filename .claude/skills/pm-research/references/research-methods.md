# Research Methods Reference

## Competitive Analysis Framework

### Porter's Five Forces (Quick Assessment)
1. **Competitive Rivalry** — How intense is competition?
2. **Supplier Power** — Key dependencies and lock-in?
3. **Buyer Power** — How easy to switch?
4. **Threat of Substitution** — Alternative approaches?
5. **Threat of New Entry** — Barriers to entry?

### Feature Comparison Matrix

| Feature | Our Product | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Feature 1 | Planned | Yes | No |
| Pricing | $X/mo | $Y/mo | $Z/mo |
| Target Audience | SMB | Enterprise | Prosumer |

### Positioning Map
Place competitors on 2 axes:
- X: Price (low to high)
- Y: Feature breadth (narrow to comprehensive)
Identify gaps = opportunities.

## Market Sizing Methodology

### Top-Down (TAM → SAM → SOM)
1. TAM: Total industry revenue or user count (from market reports)
2. SAM: TAM * % matching your geography/segment/capability
3. SOM: SAM * realistic market share (typically 1-5% for new entrants)

### Bottom-Up (SOM → SAM → TAM)
1. Count reachable customers in year 1
2. Multiply by expected revenue per customer
3. Scale up to full addressable market

## Research Quality Checklist

- [ ] Multiple sources consulted (not single-source conclusions)
- [ ] Data freshness noted (publication dates)
- [ ] Assumptions clearly labeled
- [ ] Market size estimates show methodology
- [ ] Competitor data verified from official sources where possible
